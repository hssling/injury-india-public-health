# Research Questions
## Burden of Injuries in India — Structured Research Questions

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** 2026-04-23  

---

## Primary Research Question

**RQ0 (Primary):** Which Indian states have the greatest injury burden when both premature mortality (YLLs) and disability (YLDs) are considered, and how do state priorities change when burden is assessed using DALYs or YLDs rather than deaths alone?

---

## Secondary Research Questions

**RQ1 — National Burden:**  
What is the magnitude and trend (2000–2021) of the overall injury burden in India as measured by deaths, DALYs, YLLs, and YLDs?

**RQ2 — State Heterogeneity:**  
What is the extent of state-level variation in injury burden (deaths, DALYs, YLLs, YLDs) across Indian states and union territories?

**RQ3 — Fatal vs Non-Fatal Decomposition:**  
What proportion of total injury DALYs is attributable to premature mortality (YLL) versus disability (YLD) at the national and state levels, and does this proportion vary significantly by cause?

**RQ4 — Hidden Disability Burden:**  
Which Indian states have disproportionately high disability burden (YLD rate) relative to their mortality burden (death rate), constituting a "hidden disability burden" that may be underrecognized by death-centric surveillance?

**RQ5 — Cause-Specific Priorities:**  
Which injury causes contribute the most to total DALYs, YLLs, and YLDs at the national and state levels, and does the ranking of causes differ between fatal and non-fatal burden metrics?

**RQ6 — Sex and Age Gradients:**  
How does injury burden differ by sex and age group at the national and state levels, and what is the proportion of burden falling in productive age groups (15–49 and 15–64 years)?

**RQ7 — Inequality:**  
Has the inter-state inequality in injury burden (DALY rates) increased or decreased between 2000 and 2021, and which states have experienced the greatest absolute and relative changes?

**RQ8 — Surveillance–Burden Mismatch:**  
To what extent do official administrative surveillance systems (MoRTH road accidents, NCRB accidental deaths) produce state and cause prioritization rankings that differ from rankings derived from GBD 2021 health burden estimates?

**RQ9 — Policy Typology:**  
Can a practical, evidence-based typology of Indian states be constructed that guides differentiated public health intervention priorities (mortality reduction vs. disability prevention vs. rehabilitation)?

---

## Analytic Hypotheses (Pre-Specified)

**H1:** States with high road injury mortality burden (e.g., high-NHAI states) will not necessarily rank highest on YLD-based burden, because non-transport injury mechanisms (falls, self-harm, burns) contribute disproportionately to disability.

**H2:** Administrative surveillance (MoRTH, NCRB) will underrepresent the disability burden of self-harm and falls relative to GBD estimates because surveillance focuses on deaths.

**H3:** Inter-state inequality in injury DALY rates will have declined from 2000 to 2021 due to relative improvements in high-burden states (convergence hypothesis), but will remain substantial.

**H4:** Productive-age males (15–49) will account for a majority of injury DALYs, with road injuries and self-harm being dominant causes in this group.

**H5:** Several states in central and eastern India will have high YLD:YLL ratios due to high burden of falls and occupational injuries relative to their road injury fatality burden.

## Non-Questions (Scope Boundaries)

The following questions are **outside scope** for this analysis:
- Individual-level risk factors for injury (requires survey data)
- Causal pathways between urbanization and injury burden (ecological data only)
- Cost-effectiveness of specific interventions
- Future projections of injury burden
- Clinical outcomes or case fatality ratios
- Hospital-level trauma data
