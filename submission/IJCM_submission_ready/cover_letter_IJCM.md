Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript titled **"Hidden disability within India's injury burden: a state-level public health analysis using GBD 2021"** [alternate title for IJCM] for consideration as an Original Article in the Indian Journal of Community Medicine.

**Relevance to community medicine readership:**

This study addresses a core community medicine concern: the gap between injury mortality data (used in surveillance) and the true population-level disability burden of injuries. From a preventive and social medicine perspective, deaths from injuries represent the visible tip of a much larger iceberg of non-fatal morbidity and disability.

Our analysis quantifies — for each Indian state separately — how much of the injury burden is "hidden" from administrative systems that focus on deaths. This has direct relevance for:
- Community-level injury prevention programs
- District health planning
- Primary health care capacity for injury follow-up and rehabilitation
- School and occupational safety programs

**Key adaptations for IJCM:**

This manuscript emphasizes community medicine framing: population-attributable burden, primary and secondary prevention implications, community-based rehabilitation needs, and the importance of family and community-level injury burden. The analysis is ecologically grounded in state-level public health data accessible to community medicine practitioners and planners.

**Declarations:**

- No specific funding
- Public secondary data only — no ethics approval required

We believe this study contributes meaningfully to the evidence base for community medicine and public health practice in India.
