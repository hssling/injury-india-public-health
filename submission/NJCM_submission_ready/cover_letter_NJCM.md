Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript titled **"State-level inequality in injury burden in India: a secondary-data public health analysis"** [alternate title for NJCM] for consideration as an Original Article in the National Journal of Community Medicine.

**Relevance and rationale:**

NJCM's commitment to open-access community medicine scholarship makes it an ideal home for this analysis of injury burden at the national and state level in India. Our study uses freely available secondary data (GBD 2021, MoRTH, NCRB) and open-source analytical methods — fully consistent with NJCM's emphasis on transparent, reproducible, and policy-relevant community health research.

The study fills a documented gap: while road accident mortality receives substantial attention in India, the disability burden of injuries — from falls, burns, self-harm, and poisoning — remains underquantified and underaddressed. This is particularly relevant for community medicine practitioners who see the consequences of non-fatal injury in primary care settings, community health programs, and rehabilitation services.

**Open access value-add:** All data, code, and processed outputs are available for replication, enabling community medicine educators and researchers across India to extend this work to their own state or regional contexts.

**Declarations:**
- Original, unpublished work not under concurrent review
- No competing interests
- No external funding
- Publicly available secondary data only — no ethics approval required

We hope this submission will be of value to NJCM readers and the broader community medicine community.
