We are pleased to submit our manuscript titled **"Burden of injuries in India from a public health perspective: state-level fatal–non-fatal decomposition, inequality, and surveillance–burden mismatch using GBD 2021 and official Indian secondary data"** for consideration as an Original Article in the Indian Journal of Medical Research.

**Why this study matters for IJMR and Indian public health:**

Injuries are consistently identified as a leading but underaddressed cause of the burden of disease in India. Current public health surveillance and national programme planning for injuries is largely anchored to mortality and road accident counts — a framework that may systematically underestimate the contribution of non-fatal disability to the total injury burden. This study directly addresses that gap.

Using the Global Burden of Disease Study 2021 estimates for all Indian states and union territories, complemented by official MoRTH and NCRB administrative data, we:

1. Quantify state-level injury burden using DALYs, YLLs, and YLDs simultaneously — enabling fatal vs. non-fatal decomposition across India's states.
2. Construct a novel Hidden Disability Burden Index that identifies states where disability burden substantially exceeds what mortality statistics suggest.
3. Measure inter-state inequality in injury burden over a two-decade period (2000–2021).
4. Provide the first systematic, rank-based surveillance–burden mismatch analysis comparing official Indian injury surveillance with GBD modelled estimates.

The findings have direct implications for India's National Health Policy, Road Safety Policy, rehabilitation infrastructure planning, and the integration of burden-of-disease metrics into public health programme design.
